Code With Us

Code With Us is a new way for the B.C. public sector to access talented developers and pay for code. Post an opportunity worth up to $10,000, evaluate proposals, choose a developer, and get to work!


What you need

To manage a Code With Us opportunity, you need:

  1. Clearance to share your code under an open license. You may need to consult with your intellectual property program. Read the B.C. Government’s Guide for Open-Source Projects.
  2. A technical contact - a person on your team who has the expertise and time to respond to questions and give direction to developers publicly on GitHub for the duration of the opportunity.

Get started

  1. If you don't already have a GitHub account, create one.
  2. Sign up on the BCDevExchange.
  3. Create a profile for your program and project. This helps developers understand what you’re working on, and may entice more people to apply on the opportunity.

Posting an opportunity

Create an opportunity and define what you need. Choose a fixed price for the work, and state the criteria you’ll use to choose a developer. Once you’ve got everything set, publish it to the world!

Assigning a developer

Evaluate the proposals you get and assign a developer to do the work.

If the assignment doesn’t work out by the milestone, you can reassign the work to someone else. Or, if unexpected things come up and the developer just needs a bit more time to completed the work, you can extend the date.

After the work has been completed to your satisfaction, and you’ve merged the code, the developer can send you an invoice to get paid.

Terms

Read the legal terms which govern your interactions while you manage the opportunity.

Accepting the work

Merge the code into your master if it meets the acceptance criteria. In the comments section of the issue, notify the developer you have accepted his or her code. Close the issue in GitHub.

Paying the developer

Ask the developer to send you an invoice. Provide necessary forms if they have selected payment by cheque or electronic fund transfer. Notify your Expense Authority that you merged the code.

Frequently Asked Questions

Engaging with developers

What does the technical contact have to do?
Your technical contact will need to be ready to respond to comments on the Issue in GitHub in a timely manner. Read the Province of B.C.'s guidelines on Citizen Engagement and Social Media for guidance on how to write comments appropriately.

How quickly should we respond to questions?
Strive to respond to new comments within the same business day. Questions received after hours should be responded to the next business day. The more responsive you are, the more likely developers will be motivated to work with you.

Other recommendations around communication...
Around 3 hours before the proposal period ends, post a courtesy comment reminding subscribers to get their proposals in on time.

Evaluating proposals

How should we evaluate proposals?
Before you look at any of the proposals, create an evaluation criteria matrix for scoring the proposals. Each person who is involved in the evaluation should independently score proposals before discussing as a group and coming to consensus.

What documentation should we keep?
Save the proposals and evaluations in a secure folder for when respondents ask for feedback, and for FOI purposes.

Working with the assigned developer

Why Code With Us?

We know there are tons of talented tech professionals in B.C. like you who never get the chance to work with public sector. We want to make it easier for more people to contribute to our digital projects and get paid - in a way that’s focused on writing code rather than contract paperwork.

Find an opportunity.

Find an opportunity that matches your skills and interest.

Talk with us on GitHub.

Ask questions and get clarity on what we want and how we want to work with you. If you have suggestions of think the price is too low, tell us!

Apply.

Log in and upload your proposal. If you’re selected, you’ll be notified and be assigned to work on the issue for a set period of time.

Do the work.

Work collaboratively and incrementally with the opportunity contact. Commit work as early and often as possible so they can ensure you’re on the right track and give direction.

Get paid.

Once the acceptance criteria is met and your code is merged, submit your invoice and expect payment within 30 days.
